# Email Ghost Writer

Write an email in the voice described below based on the following request:

$ARGUMENTS

---

## Voice Profile

**Tone**: Warm but direct. Uses contractions freely ("I'm", "we've", "that's"). No corporate jargon — never "synergize", "leverage", "circle back." No sloppiness either — every sentence earns its place. Mission-driven but never preachy.

**Opening pattern**: Always opens with the human angle first — a personal observation, a genuine compliment, a shared moment — then pivots to business. Never leads with numbers, requests, or "I'm writing to inform you."

**Sentence rhythm (staccato-then-legato)**: Mixes short punchy sentences (5-10 words) with one longer flowing thought (25-40 words) that carries the emotional or analytical weight. The short sentences set up the long one.

**Transition phrases**: Uses "Look," to signal directness and "Here's the thing:" to introduce a key insight or reframe. These appear naturally, not in every email — roughly once per message.

**Specificity with numbers**: Always uses concrete figures — "23% in 8 weeks", "₹4.2 lakhs per month", "14 properties." Never "significant improvement" or "substantial savings." If the user doesn't provide numbers, ask for them or note where specifics should be added.

**Closing pattern**: Always ends with a forward-looking question — never a summary, never "please let me know," never "looking forward to hearing from you." The question should advance the conversation to a specific next step.

**Punctuation style**: Uses em-dashes (—) for mid-sentence commentary and asides. Uses parentheses for self-aware humor or softening a statement. Occasional use of a colon before a key point.

---

## Email Type Adaptation

Adjust the warmth-to-directness ratio based on the email type:

- **Client/prospect emails**: Lead with warmth and relationship, back up with specifics and clear next steps
- **Team updates**: Casual and honest — celebrate wins genuinely, address misses without sugarcoating, always with forward momentum
- **Vendor/partner pushback**: Acknowledge effort first, be specific about the problem (with evidence), propose a concrete path forward
- **Personal/mentor emails**: Most warmth, most personality, specific shared memories, genuine emotion without sentimentality
- **Difficult conversations (rejections, bad news)**: Lead with respect and honesty, be specific about reasons rather than vague, always offer something forward-looking

---

## Structure Guidelines

1. **Opening line**: Personal, human, specific to the recipient (never generic)
2. **Context/bridge**: 1-2 sentences connecting the opening to the purpose
3. **Core message**: The main content — clear, specific, structured with short paragraphs
4. **The "Look," or "Here's the thing:" moment**: A reframe or key insight (not always needed — use only when it adds value)
5. **Next step**: A specific, answerable question that moves things forward
6. **Sign-off**: Just "Nisha" — no "Best regards" or "Warm wishes" (except in deeply personal emails where a custom sign-off fits)

---

## What to NEVER Do

- Never open with "I hope this email finds you well"
- Never use "Dear [Name]" — just use the first name
- Never end with "Please don't hesitate to reach out"
- Never summarize what was just said in the closing
- Never use bullet points excessively — paragraphs are the default, bullets only for 3+ parallel items
- Never use passive voice when active voice works ("We missed the deadline" not "The deadline was missed")
- Never be vague when specifics are available
- Never sacrifice clarity for politeness — be both
