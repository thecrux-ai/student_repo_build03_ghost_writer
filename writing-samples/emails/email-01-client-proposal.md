# Email 01 — Client Proposal

**Context**: Nisha is emailing Rajesh Menon, VP of Operations at a 200-person logistics company, after an initial discovery call. This is a formal proposal to pilot GreenGrid's energy management system across their 3 Mumbai warehouses.

---

**From**: Nisha Kapoor
**To**: Rajesh Menon
**Subject**: GreenGrid pilot for Apex Logistics — what 3 warehouses could look like

---

Rajesh,

It was genuinely great speaking with you on Tuesday. The part where you described walking through your Andheri warehouse at 2 AM and seeing every light blazing with nobody on the floor — that stuck with me. That's the exact moment most of our customers describe when they realize something needs to change.

Here's what I'm proposing for Apex.

We run a 90-day pilot across your three Mumbai warehouses — Andheri, Powai, and Vikhroli. Our system installs in under a week per site (no downtime, no rewiring), and within 30 days you'll have a live dashboard showing exactly where energy is being wasted and why.

The numbers from similar deployments: our logistics clients typically see 18-24% energy reduction in the first quarter. For your square footage, that's roughly ₹4.2 lakhs per month in savings across all three sites. The pilot costs ₹6 lakhs total — so you'd break even before the pilot ends.

Look, I know you mentioned your board is cautious about new vendors right now. Totally fair. That's why the pilot is structured as a proof-of-value — we only bill for the second and third months if month one hits the agreed benchmarks. If we miss, you owe us nothing.

I've attached the full proposal with technical specs, timeline, and the benchmark framework. My team can have Andheri instrumented by March 15th if we get sign-off by next Friday (and yes, we've actually hit that timeline before — I wouldn't promise it otherwise).

What does your calendar look like next week to walk through the benchmark targets together?

Nisha
