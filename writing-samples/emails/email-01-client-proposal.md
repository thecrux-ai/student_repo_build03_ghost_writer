# Email 01 — Acme Renewal Pitch

**Context**: Arjun is emailing Deepak Sharma, VP of Operations at Acme Corp — their largest client (INR 45 lakhs annually). Contract expires March 31. Deepak wants progress on mobile performance and pricing before mid-February, or they start evaluating CompetitorX. Arjun needs to make the case for renewal while being honest about timelines.

---

**From**: Arjun Mehta
**To**: Deepak Sharma
**Subject**: Acme renewal — the fixes, the pricing, and what I need from you

---

Deepak,

Three numbers to start: your ops team saves 8 hours a week on reporting, your dashboard loads 40% faster since our Q4 update, and you've got 4 API integrations running with zero custom dev work. I'm leading with these because the renewal conversation should start from what's working, not just what's broken.

Now the honest part.

Mobile performance — you're right, and we should have fixed this sooner. I've already pulled Suresh's engineering team onto it. Here's what I need you to know: the Android crash rate is down from 2-3x daily to under 1x as of last week (we pushed a patch on January 28). The full stability fix ships February 15 — that's a hard commitment, not a target. Anita's team will have a staging build to test by February 10.

Pricing — I hear you on the CompetitorX quote. Just being transparent: their enterprise tier at 30% less doesn't include API marketplace access (separate add-on), dedicated CSM (extra fee), or scheduled custom dashboards (basic CSV export only). Apples to apples, the gap is closer to 8-10%, not 30%.

That said, I'm not going to sit here and argue that our pricing is perfect. Here's what I'm proposing:

- A loyalty adjustment for Year 3+ customers (which Acme qualifies for)
- Early access to our self-serve onboarding tools launching in Q2 (Project Alpha — this will save your team significant setup time on new workflows)
- Priority SLA on the mobile fixes with weekly progress updates directly to Anita

My read: we can save this account. The value is there — INR 62 lakhs in recovered time and productivity annually against a INR 45 lakh contract. The mobile issue was real, and I own the fact that we were slow to act on it. But switching costs you 6-8 weeks of engineering migration plus the productivity hit, and you'd lose the integrations your team already relies on.

Can we get 30 minutes in the first week of February? I want to walk through the pricing options with you directly — no sales deck, just the numbers.

Arjun
