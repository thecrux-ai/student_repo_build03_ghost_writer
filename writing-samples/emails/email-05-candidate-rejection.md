# Email 05 — Candidate Rejection

**Context**: Nisha is rejecting a strong candidate (Meera Joshi) who made it to the final round for a Growth Marketing Manager role. Meera was good but not the right fit for the specific role. Nisha wants to be empathetic and keep the door open — she might have a different role in 6 months.

---

**From**: Nisha Kapoor
**To**: Meera Joshi
**Subject**: Your GreenGrid interview — an honest update

---

Meera,

I want to start by saying something most rejection emails don't: you were genuinely impressive in this process. Your case study presentation — particularly the part where you broke down the funnel analysis for that EdTech campaign and identified the Stage 2 drop-off that everyone else missed — that was sharp, original thinking. Three people on my team commented on it afterward.

I also want to be honest rather than vague, because you deserve better than "we decided to go in a different direction."

We offered the role to another candidate whose experience is specifically in enterprise B2B energy sales — 7 years of it, with a track record in the exact market segment GreenGrid is targeting in the next two quarters. Your consumer and EdTech growth background is genuinely strong, and in a different quarter with different priorities, this conversation would be different. This wasn't a close-but-no — this was a "right person, wrong moment" situation.

Here's the thing: I don't say "let's stay in touch" unless I mean it. We're planning to open a Product Marketing lead role in Q4, and your blend of analytical depth and storytelling ability is exactly what that role needs. I'd want you in that pipeline if you're interested.

Two things I'd suggest in the meantime — and I'm offering these because your work genuinely impressed me, not as a consolation. First, Ankita Rao at TerraWatt is building out her growth team and I think you'd be a strong fit. I'm happy to make the intro if you'd like. Second, the climate-tech growth community (I'll forward the Slack invite) is where half the interesting roles surface before they hit LinkedIn.

Would you be open to a quick call in September when we start scoping the Product Marketing role? And shall I reach out to Ankita on your behalf?

Rooting for you genuinely,

Nisha
